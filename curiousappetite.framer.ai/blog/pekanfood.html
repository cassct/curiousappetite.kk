Tucked away along the lively Concubine Lane, Pekan Food is one of those cafés that’s easy to walk past but impossible to forget once you step in. The space carries a rustic, cozy vibe, perfect for a relaxed meal or a casual catch-up. Bonus points: it’s pork-free and Muslim-friendly, making it a welcoming spot for everyone.

🤤Here’s what we tried:

1. Hawaiian Chicken Burger – Juicy grilled chicken topped with sweet pineapple and greens, all sandwiched in a soft, lightly toasted bun. Each bite had a great balance of savory and sweet.

2. Fried Cajun Chicken – Crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, with just the right hint of spice. It comes with yogurt sauce on the side that complements the spice perfectly.

3. Beef Strips Fettuccine Alfredo – Creamy, rich, and indulgent. The beef was perfectly seared, and the pasta had the right bite to carry the delicious sauce.

4. Mushroom Soup – Smooth, earthy, and flavorful – a simple starter done really well.

They also had a selection of really cool drinks like milkshakes, though we were too full to try them this time. 🥹

Overall, Pekan Food serves exceptionally tasty dishes in a charming, low-key setting. Definitely a spot to check out if you want hearty, satisfying food away from the usual tourist hustle. 🙌🏻

Business Hours: Opens everyday from 4:00PM to 1:00AM.

Highly recommended!
